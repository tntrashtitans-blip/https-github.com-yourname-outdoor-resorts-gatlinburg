# SEO Guidelines for Local Website Builds

Source reviewed: Sarvesh Shrivastava X thread, mirrored at Thread Navigator.

These rules apply when building OutdoorResortsGatlinburg.com pages and can be reused for future local/service/rental sites.

## Core Approach

- Treat SEO as a site architecture and trust-building system, not a keyword-stuffing exercise.
- Build pages around real user intent: guests planning stays, families comparing lodging, people looking near attractions, and owners/investors researching rental opportunities.
- Use the business's actual location, amenities, photos, listings, reviews, FAQs, and nearby landmarks as the raw material for page copy.
- Write pages that could help a real visitor decide what to do next, then optimize headings, links, metadata, and schema around that purpose.

## Page Planning

Each page should have one clear job:

- Home page: explain the resort, rentals, rentable lots, location, and major paths.
- Rentals page: help guests compare available RV rentals and rentable RV lots with booking/availability links.
- Stay guide: answer practical questions about staying at Outdoor Resorts Gatlinburg.
- Gatlinburg camping: capture RV camping and cabin-alternative searches while clearly saying tent camping is not offered.
- Great Smoky Mountains page: prioritize nearby Greenbrier/east Gatlinburg park access before broader Smokies content.
- Dollywood page: help guests plan theme-park days from ORG.
- Rocky Top Sports World page: serve tournament families looking for lodging near the venue.
- Dining page: recommend restaurants using ORG's East Parkway address as the starting point.
- Owner/investor pages: speak to RV lot owners, future buyers, rental setup, consulting, and real estate conversations.

## Local SEO Content Rules

- Mention the exact location where useful: Outdoor Resorts Gatlinburg, 4229 East Parkway, Gatlinburg, TN 37738.
- Use nearby landmarks and drive context naturally, not as random keyword lists.
- Prefer specific phrases over generic ones: "RV camping near Gatlinburg," "RV rentals at Outdoor Resorts Gatlinburg," "rentable RV lots," "bring your own camper," "near Rocky Top Sports World."
- Do not invent amenities, access rules, or policies.
- Do not imply tent camping is available. If tent camping comes up, say it is not offered.
- Use real ORG/resort/listing images wherever possible.

## Page Structure

Every major page should include:

- A clear H1 tied to the page intent.
- A short intro that says who the page helps and why ORG matters.
- Useful subsections with descriptive H2s.
- Internal links to related pages.
- A CTA matched to the page: browse rentals, read stay guide, list your RV, or explore ownership.
- FAQs when the page answers common planning questions.

## Internal Linking

- Use internal links to connect pages by visitor journey, not just nav repetition.
- Link from attraction pages back to rentals and stay guide.
- Link from rentals to stay guide and list-your-RV.
- Link camping content to rentals and rentable lots.
- Link owner/investor pages to list-your-RV and future units-for-sale content.
- Use descriptive anchors such as "browse Outdoor Resorts Gatlinburg rentals" rather than vague "click here."

## Trust Signals

- Use real property names, listing platforms, and listing types.
- Distinguish Airbnb/VRBO RV rentals from Hipcamp or other vacant lot rentals.
- Make owner/consulting content transparent and separate from guest trip-planning content.
- Include address and location details prominently where they help users.
- Avoid generic stock-like claims; use details a guest or owner can verify.

## Metadata and Schema

For each finished page, add:

- Unique title tag.
- Unique meta description.
- Canonical URL placeholder or final URL.
- Open Graph title/description/image when the page has a share-worthy image.
- LocalBusiness/LodgingBusiness-style schema where appropriate.
- FAQPage schema only when visible FAQs are actually on the page.
- ItemList schema for rental listings only when the page lists real units/lots.

## Copy Style

- Keep the resort information page as the main concept; rentals support the resort, not the other way around.
- Use "camping" strategically for SEO, but frame it as RV camping/campground-style stays.
- Write in a helpful, specific, local tone.
- Avoid weak filler like "sports travel," "area attractions," or generic "things to do" copy without context.
- Avoid awkward hanging lines in cards, rows, headlines, and buttons. Write copy to fit the design.

## Build Checklist for New Pages

Before considering a page done:

- Does the page answer one clear search intent?
- Does it use ORG-specific details, not generic Gatlinburg filler?
- Does it link naturally to rentals or the next useful page?
- Are photos real and relevant to the page topic?
- Is there a clear CTA?
- Are title, meta description, H1, H2s, and internal links aligned?
- Is the page useful even if Google did not exist?
