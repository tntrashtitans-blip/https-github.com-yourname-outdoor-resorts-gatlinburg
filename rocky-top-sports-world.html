Resort Amenities

Everything a tournament family actually needs

Outdoor Resorts Gatlinburg is a full-amenity resort — not just a campground. Here's what's waiting when your team gets off the field.

🏊

Two swimming pools

Nothing recovers tired legs faster than a pool. After back-to-back tournament games in Tennessee heat, ORG has two on-site pools — one open to all resort guests, and a smaller adult-only pool for parents who want a quieter place to decompress between games.

🍳

Full kitchens in most units

Tournament travel is expensive. Full kitchens let you skip the restaurants for breakfast and post-game dinners — cutting your food budget dramatically compared to a hotel stay. Cook team meals, pack lunches for the fields, and manage dietary needs on your own schedule.

🛝

Playground for the little ones

Not every kid in your party is on the roster. ORG's on-site playground gives younger siblings something to do while the team rests up for the next day's games. No hotel lobby, no bored kids.

🎮

Game room

The evening after a tournament day, players need somewhere to decompress. The resort game room is a team favorite — a social space that keeps the energy alive without anyone having to go anywhere.

⛳

Putt-putt course

A resort classic that works for any age. Tournament families with mixed ages love having on-site activities that don't require a car, a reservation, or another credit card swipe. Just walk out and play.

🎣

On-site fishing pond

The resort's stocked fishing pond is a quiet counterpoint to tournament intensity. Parents, siblings, and players who need a mental reset find the pond one of the most unexpected amenities at ORG — and one of the most appreciated.

🧺

On-site laundry

Multi-day tournaments mean dirty uniforms. ORG's laundry facility means you're not hand-washing jerseys in a hotel sink or paying for a laundromat run. Clean uniforms for game two. Essential for any weekend tournament.

🔥

Outdoor space & fire pits

Hotel rooms don't have outdoor space. ORG lots do — awnings, outdoor seating, and fire pits that turn an evening into something the team will actually remember. Cookouts, team meetings, or just unwinding under the Smoky Mountain sky.

About the Complex

Rocky Top Sports World — Gatlinburg's premier tournament destination

Rocky Top Sports World is one of the Southeast's largest and most modern multi-sport complexes, drawing thousands of tournament families to Gatlinburg every year. The facility hosts youth and adult tournaments across soccer, baseball, softball, lacrosse, volleyball, basketball, and more — running nearly every weekend throughout the spring, summer, and fall seasons.

The complex sits on East Parkway — the same road that runs directly past Outdoor Resorts Gatlinburg. That makes ORG one of the most logistically convenient lodging options available: no navigating downtown Gatlinburg traffic, no figuring out alternate routes, just a straight shot on the same road your team is already on.

Tournament weekends fill Gatlinburg hotels fast. Booking an ORG rental early gives your team a confirmed place to stay that's better-equipped, more spacious, and more affordable than anything with a hotel flag on East Parkway.

Common Questions

Lodging near Rocky Top Sports World — FAQ

How far is Outdoor Resorts Gatlinburg from Rocky Top Sports World?

Approximately 4 miles — about 10 minutes by car along East Parkway. It's one of the closest lodging options to the complex, and you avoid the congestion of downtown Gatlinburg entirely.

Can multiple families book next to each other?

Yes, in many cases. Browse available rentals and check with individual owners about adjacent availability. Tournament groups often book a cluster of nearby units for the community feel that hotels simply can't provide.

Are RV rentals at ORG kid-friendly?

Very much so. The resort has a playground, pools, putt-putt, a game room, and a fishing pond — all on-site. It's a significantly better environment for kids of all ages than a standard hotel stay.

Do the rental units have kitchens?

Most units at ORG include a full kitchen — refrigerator, stove, microwave, and cooking basics. This is one of the biggest cost advantages over hotel lodging for tournament families who want to manage their own meals and budget.

What is there to do after the games?

Sit around a fire pit, cook dinner at your own site, let the kids run to the pond while the adults decompress at the adult pool. Tournament weekends are exhausting — ORG gives families a place to actually recover together, without getting back in the car.

How do I book a rental at Outdoor Resorts Gatlinburg?

Browse the rentals page for current listings. Units are listed on Airbnb, VRBO, Hipcamp, and sometimes directly through owners. Book early — tournament weekends fill quickly throughout the spring and fall seasons.
